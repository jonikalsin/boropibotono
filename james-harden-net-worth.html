James Harden | Career

Harden started his school basketball career playing for Artesia High School. Here, he performed brilliantly and won notable honors.

Likewise, after a successful school career, he joined Arizona State University and played for the Sun Devils.

Meanwhile, as a freshman, he grabbed 17.8 points, 5.3 rebounds, and 3.2 assists per game. After his brilliant display at College, he was drafted in the 2009 NBA Draft.

Professional Career

He was selected as the overall third pick in the 2009 NBA Draft by the Oklahoma Thunders and played for thunders till 2012.

During his first season stay at Thunders, he recorded the fourth-highest 3 point percentage in NBA history as a U-21 player. Here, he was named NBA All-Rookie second team.

Likewise, he excelled in his performance in the next season, where he scored 10 plus points on 54 occasions.

In the 2011-12 season, Harden averaged 16.8 points, 4.1 rebounds, and 3.7 assists in 62 games. For his excellent play, he received the NBA Sixth Man of the Year Award.

Meanwhile, in 2012, he joined Houston Rockets and immediately impacted with his game for the Rockets.

In his first season, he made a record by scoring 37 points, a career-high 12 assists, and six rebounds against Detroit Pistons.

Surprisingly, in his second for Rockets, he outperformed his first game by scoring 45 points against Atlanta Hawks.

He had a phenomenal first season at Rockets, where he averaged 25.9 points, 4.9 rebounds, and 5.8 assists in 78 games.

The following season, he became only the third player in history to grab 3000 points in the first 120 games playing for the Rockets. The same year, he earned All-NBA First Team Honors.

In the next season, he continued to perform like where he left last year. Later, he won the National Basketball Players Association’s MVP for the 2014-15 season.

Meanwhile, he continued to perform outstanding throughout his career while playing for the Rockets. Here, he played for them till 2020.

After parting ways with the Rockets, he joined Brooklyn Nets in 2021. He averages 20.2 points per game in 9 games.

National Team Career

Harden represents the United States of America as his national basketball team. While playing for the country, he has won a gold medal at the 2012 London Olympics.

Likewise, he has won a gold medal at the 2014 World Cup held in Spain.
